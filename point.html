The Big Three of Understanding Game Design






Number One: Mechanics that work with the style and feel of the game


This one is super important. If your game mechanics dont fit your style of game,
then it will feel wrong to use those mechanics and the player will have a bad time playing your game.

For example: A infinite ammo perk in a survival horror game.


Number Two: Levels or encounters that push you into said mechanics


This point works as a follow-up to point one. Games should structure encounters that push you to use the games mechanics.
If this doesn't happen then the player will never use these cool mechanics.

For example: Being given a double jump mechanic
but every encounter and/or bossfight has attacks where you can avoid them just by moving over without jumping.


Number Three: A sense of player progression that happens as the game goes on


Point three is probably the most important one. Games should have the player feel like the game is progressing.
What that means is that the player should be getting stronger and more skilled,
however more enemies and encounters should take place to test the players ablilities.

For example: The game having a system of uprades that make your current ablilities stronger.
